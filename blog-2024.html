  • Kudimiyampalayam, Erode – April 2024

SAMARTH Training for Silk Handloom Weaving

The SAMARTH program trained 30 individuals in traditional silk handloom weaving techniques. Among them, 18 candidates secured placement in reputed handloom companies, while 12 participants became self-employed by setting up looms at their homes.

This initiative led to an impressive 60% increase in income levels, offering sustainable livelihood and empowering rural communities in the textile sector.

  • SKILL DEVELOPMENT
  • Dhasapagoundan Puthur, Erode – April 2024

SAMARTH Training – Traditional Weaving Meets Modern Techniques

A total of 30 candidates received training in silk handloom weaving, preserving traditional skills while learning modern enhancements. Out of them, 11 were placed in handloom companies earning up to ₹14,000/month, 9 upgraded their looms with advanced techniques, and 10 became self-employed with home-based units.

The program resulted in a significant 60–70% income boost, creating long-term sustainable opportunities for rural artisans.

  • SKILL RECOGNITION
  • Salem – 23 June 2024

SAMARTH Certificate Distribution Ceremony

SBST distributed certificates to 82 candidates who completed the SAMARTH handloom weaving training in Salem. The certificates are QR code-enabled to ensure authenticity and traceability.

Out of the 82 trained: 58 were placed in handloom weaving companies, 12 upgraded their looms with advanced techniques, and 12 became self-employed with home setups.

This initiative enhances skill credibility, promotes socio-economic development, and strengthens the future of the handloom industry in Tamil Nadu.
