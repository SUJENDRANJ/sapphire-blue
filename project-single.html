Kalaignar Kaivinai Thittam (KKT) – Textile Startup Skill Program

The Kalaignar Kaivinai Thittam provides skill development in advanced tailoring, embroidery, weaving, and traditional textile printing to promote both entrepreneurship and employment in Tamil Nadu’s textile sector.

Project Overview

A comprehensive textile startup skill program equipping participants with advanced tailoring, embroidery, weaving, and block-printing expertise, along with entrepreneurship support.

Associated with

Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME)
District Industry Centre (DIC)
Tamil Nadu Skill Development Corporation (TNSDC)

Objectives

Develop advanced textile-related skills
Enable trainees to launch startups and pursue skilled employment
Provide financial linkage with MSME/DIC for small business growth

Activities

Training programs in tailoring, embroidery, weaving, and block printing
Provision of stipends, free meals, and certification upon completion
Entrepreneurship guidance and loan facilitation

Impact

Significant rise in self-employment and income among rural women and youth through startup creation and skilled placements.

SAMARTH - Capacity Building & Skill Development Training for Silk Handloom Weaving & Block Printing

The SAMARTH program focuses on preserving and promoting traditional silk handloom weaving and hand block printing across Tamil Nadu. Conducted in partnership with the Central Silk Board, Ministry of Textiles, it empowers rural artisians through structured skill development programs.

Project Overview

Preserving endangered weaving techniques, certifying artisians, and creating direct market linkages for sustainable livelihoods.

Associated with

Central Silk Board, Ministry of Textiles, Government of India

Objectives

Revive endangered handloom weaving techniques and crafts
Provide certified training for sustainable livelihoods
Create direct market linkage for artisians

Activities

Weaving & block-printing training sessions across Tamil Nadu
Certificate distributions with QR-validation
Post-training support for employment or entrepreneurship

Impact

Hundreds of artisians have been trained, with many achieving significant income growth through employment and entrepreneurship.

Herbal Gardens in Schools

SBST has established herbal gardens in government schools across Erode district, in partnership with the Ministry of Ayush. This initiative blends environmental sustainability with traditional Ayurvedic knowledge.

Project Overview

Creating living classrooms that educate students on native medicinal plants and Ayurveda, while fostering practical environmental stewardship.

Associated with

Ministry of Ayush, Government of India

Objectives

Establish herbal gardens in schools to serve educational and medicinal purposes
Promote awareness of Ayurveda and traditional Indian medicine among students
Cultivate environmental responsibility and appreciation of biodiversity among school children

Activities

Identify and select suitable schools across Erode district
Establish and maintain herbal garden plots with local medicinal species
Conduct interactive awareness sessions for students and teachers about plant uses

Impact

Enhanced eco-awareness and traditional medicinal knowledge among hundreds of school children, fostering a lifelong respect for nature and Ayurveda.
